# Knowledge Graphs

## Introduction

A Knowledge Graph is a way of representing information using entities and relationships. It helps in storing and organizing data in a structured form.

Knowledge Graphs represent information as nodes and edges.

Example:

Student → studies → Artificial Intelligence

Here:

* Student is an entity
* Artificial Intelligence is an entity
* studies is the relationship

## Features

* Represents relationships between entities
* Easy visualization of connected information
* Supports reasoning and inference
* Improves search and recommendation systems

## Applications

* Search Engines
* Recommendation Systems
* Healthcare
* Social Networks
* Question Answering Systems

## Advantages

* Better data organization
* Easy integration of information
* Improved decision making
* Supports semantic search

## Conclusion

Knowledge Graphs provide an effective method for representing and connecting information. They are widely used in modern AI systems and data-driven applications.
